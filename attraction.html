PINNAWALA ELEPHANT ORPHANAGE

For all you elephant lover, Pinnawala elephant orphanage is the best place to go to a unique never before face on experience with elephants like no other. This unique sanctuary is home to many elephants where they are taken care of. This orphanage does not just have local reach but its well known all over the world.

This great sanctuary is a huge remote area filled with many trees,shruds,herbs so the elephants can feast all they want.93 elephants currently inhabit these grounds where they are taken care of. Maya oya being located close by brings an additional value to this beautiful habitat.

Located in Rambukkana,in Kegalle city, established by the Sri Lanka Wildlife department, covers almost 24 acres. Also know as the best herd of elephants in captivity all around the world is also a breeding place for elephants where they live peacefully. Tourists can feed the baby elephants, join the morning and afternoon baths and enjoy a good full day among them.

SINHARAJA

Sinharaja is the largest tropical rainforest situated in the South East Sri Lanka. It is home to many species such as birds, mammals, butterflies, rare amphibians, insects and reptiles. It has an area of 18,900 acres with an altitude from 300m to 1,170m. There are two main natural trails in the Sinharaja forest, they are, the one that leads to Moulawella and another one that leads to Sinhagala.

SPIDERS

Even though people are scared of spiders, only a few species are dangerous and they only attack if threatens. Spiders are both carnivorous and venomous. Spiders use their venom to paralize its pray. One of the most dangerous spiders is the Black widow. Even though they have a powerful venom, they are naturally shy and timid. Spiders habit deserts, forests and even backyards. Some of the most common types of siders in Sri Lanka are jumping spiders, common red house spiders, Asian hermit spiders, Mankulama pink-banded ornamental tarantula, and Ornate tiger spider. Out of these, the most poisonous spider is the ornate tiger spider.

BIRDS

Sri Lanka is home to 34 types of birds. The most appropriate times for bird watching are November and April. According to BirdLife International, Sri Lanka was named as one of the top endemic bird regions. Some of the most famous birds in Sri Lanka are, Sri Lankan spurfouwl, Sri Lankan wood pigeon, Sri Lankan green pigeon, Layard’s parakeet, Green-billed coucal, Sri Lanka chestnut- backed owlet. Etc
